SEMAA Emergency Response Protocol ERP-2026-01

Organization: SEMAA Marine Operations
Document Type: Operational Protocol
Owner: Safety Director Alice Chen
Effective Date: 2026-02-01
Version: 1.2

Purpose
This protocol defines mandatory response procedures for all declared emergencies aboard SEMAA vessels, including engine casualties, fire, flooding, and man-overboard events. All crew must be familiar with this document before departure clearance is granted.

Section 1. Emergency Classification
- Level 1 (Minor): Contained incident with no risk of vessel loss. Managed by chief engineer or officer on watch.
- Level 2 (Serious): Incident affecting vessel control or crew safety. Requires immediate notification to Fleet Manager Brian Wu.
- Level 3 (Critical): Imminent risk of vessel loss or crew fatality. Requires immediate activation of full emergency command chain.

Section 2. Engine Overheating Response
- Step 1: Reduce engine RPM to idle immediately upon overheating alert.
- Step 2: Chief engineer initiates cooling protocol within 7 minutes of alert.
- Step 3: If temperature does not normalize within 20 minutes, declare Level 2 emergency.
- Step 4: Submit emergency inspection request within 1 hour of docking.
- Step 5: Notify Safety Director Alice Chen via hotline +886-2-5555-1200 within 2 hours.
- Note: CPF-9 cooling pump filter blockage is the most common root cause. Ensure spare inventory is maintained per supplier contract SC-88 with OceanCore Parts Ltd.

Section 3. Fire Response
- Step 1: Sound general alarm, broadcast on VHF channel 16.
- Step 2: Officer on watch declares emergency and initiates muster.
- Step 3: First response team deploys extinguishers (reference inspection checklist IC-2026-V1 for extinguisher locations).
- Step 4: If fire is not contained within 5 minutes, issue MAYDAY on channel 16.
- Step 5: Activate EPIRB if vessel must be abandoned.

Section 4. Man Overboard
- Step 1: Shout man overboard, throw life ring immediately.
- Step 2: Officer on watch takes the helm, begins recovery maneuver.
- Step 3: Mark GPS position, broadcast on VHF channel 16.
- Step 4: Contact Incident Hotline +886-2-5555-1200.
- Step 5: Complete incident report within 24 hours (reference IR-2026 series format).

Section 5. Reporting Requirements
- All Level 2 and Level 3 incidents must be reported to Fleet Manager Brian Wu within 4 hours.
- A formal incident report following the IR-2026 format must be submitted within 48 hours.
- Emergency reserve fund activation above USD 20,000 requires notification to Safety Director within 12 hours per budget policy BP-2026-H1.

Section 6. Crew Readiness
- All crew must complete Module A (Engine Overheating Response) and Module C (Incident Communication Escalation) from the Q1 training program before being assigned emergency duties.
- Vessels with training completion below 90 percent must complete makeup drills before departure authorization.
