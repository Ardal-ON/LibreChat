SEMAA Vessel Inspection Checklist IC-2026-V1

Organization: SEMAA Marine Operations
Document Type: Standard Inspection Checklist
Owner: Fleet Manager Brian Wu
Effective Date: 2026-01-15

Purpose
This checklist applies to all scheduled and emergency inspections for Class A and Class B vessels. Inspectors must complete every section and obtain supervisor sign-off before releasing a vessel for departure.

Section 1. Hull and Structural Integrity
- Inspect hull plating for cracks, corrosion, and impact damage.
- Check keel alignment and verify no abnormal stress deformation.
- Inspect all watertight doors and confirm seals are intact.
- Verify bilge pumps are operational and bilge water level is within acceptable range.
- Document any hull coating deterioration exceeding 15 percent surface area.

Section 2. Engine and Propulsion
- Check main engine oil pressure and coolant level.
- Inspect cooling pump filters (model CPF-9) for blockage or wear.
- Test engine overheating alert threshold per monitoring system specification.
- Verify fuel lines for leaks and confirm all fittings are torqued to spec.
- Record engine hours since last service.

Section 3. Navigation and Communication Equipment
- Power on radar system and verify sweep range accuracy within 0.5 degrees.
- Test GPS fix acquisition time (must lock within 90 seconds under open sky).
- Verify VHF radio channels 16 and 70 are clear and transmitting.
- Confirm AIS transponder is broadcasting correct vessel MMSI and position.
- Test intercom system between bridge and engine room.

Section 4. Safety Equipment
- Verify all life jackets are accessible, undamaged, and within service date.
- Inspect liferaft hydrostatic release mechanism and confirm service tag is current.
- Test EPIRB activation indicator light and confirm registration is up to date.
- Count and inspect fire extinguisher units; verify pressure gauges are in green zone.
- Confirm flare kit is within expiry date and stowed in designated locker.

Section 5. Electrical Systems
- Inspect emergency power relay panel; check all terminals for tightness (reference slot E-4 from PM-2026-014 findings).
- Test emergency generator start-up time (must reach full load within 30 seconds).
- Inspect battery banks for corrosion and verify charge level above 80 percent.
- Check navigation light function: port, starboard, stern, and masthead.

Section 6. Fuel Transfer Pre-Check
- Confirm two-person crew is assigned before initiating any diesel transfer.
- Verify transfer log is available and dated for current operation.
- Inspect transfer hose for cracks and confirm all valves are correctly labeled.
- Set timer reminder at 70-minute mark per policy requirement.
